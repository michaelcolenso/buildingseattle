## Design Context

### Users

**Who they are:** Construction industry professionals operating in the Greater Seattle metro — general contractors, subcontractors, material suppliers, professional service firms (architects, engineers, estimators), and investors/developers.

**Their context:** They are busy, relationship-driven professionals who evaluate tools quickly. They visit the site during work hours, often on desktop, looking for actionable intelligence that gives them a competitive edge. They value speed, accuracy, and signals of trustworthiness over novelty.

**The job to be done:** Discover active and upcoming construction projects, identify the contractors and decision-makers attached to them, and convert that intelligence into qualified leads or bids — faster than competitors who are still browsing city portals and PDFs.

**Emotional goals:** The interface should evoke *confidence* (this data is reliable and current), *momentum* (there are real opportunities here and I can act on them now), and *competence* (this platform understands my industry and my workflow).

### Brand Personality

**Voice & tone:** Direct, knowledgeable, and unapologetically industry-focused. No fluff, no abstract corporate speak. Speak like a seasoned project manager who respects your time.

**3-word personality:** Bold, Industrial, Confident

- **Bold** — Strong typographic hierarchy, high-contrast decisions, clear CTAs that don't whisper.
- **Industrial** — Authentic material sensibility. Not "tech-bro" sleek, but built-to-last. Solid structures, clear grid systems, functional beauty.
- **Confident** — Data density handled with authority. Live indicators, real numbers, no placeholder copy. The platform knows the market and acts like it.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Premium trade intelligence. Think modern equipment manufacturer meets high-end B2B data platform. Substantial, not fragile.

**References:**
- **Caterpillar / Deere digital experiences** — rugged credibility translated into clean digital surfaces
- **Stripe** — information density handled with precision and calm authority (but warmer, less "developer tool")
- **Procore or Autodesk Construction Cloud** — industry-native patterns that feel immediately familiar to the target audience

**Anti-references:**
- Generic SaaS startup aesthetic — avoid purple gradients, abstract 3D hero illustrations, meaningless buzzword copy
- Government/city portal — no bureaucratic clutter, no 90s-era data tables as primary UI
- Overly playful/friendly — no cartoonish illustrations, bubbly rounded UI, or emoji-heavy interfaces that undermine credibility with serious professionals

**Theme:** Light mode is primary. Dark mode is supported via `prefers-color-scheme: dark` and should feel like a "night shift" view — same structure, muted but not inverted into a neon arcade.

### Design Principles

1. **Data is the hero.** Every visual decision should make permit and contractor information more scannable, trustworthy, and actionable. Decoration that doesn't serve comprehension is clutter.

2. **Built, not decorated.** Favor structural clarity over ornamentation. Use strong grids, clear hierarchy, and purposeful whitespace. The UI should feel like it was engineered, not styled.

3. **Motion with purpose.** Animations should signal state change (live pulse, hover lift, loading skeletons) or guide attention. Avoid gratuitous motion. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

4. **Industry-native patterns.** Use conventions that construction professionals already understand — status badges, project cards, contractor profiles, territory filters. Don't reinvent for novelty's sake.

5. **Confidence through specificity.** Real numbers, real neighborhoods, real contractor names. Avoid vague claims. The copy and data should demonstrate deep market knowledge.

**Typography:** System font stack (`-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", Roboto, sans-serif`). Headings at weight 700–800 with tight tracking (`-0.02em` on hero). Body at weight 400, line-height 1.6.

**Border radius scale:** `0.5rem` (8px) for buttons and small cards, `0.75rem` (12px) for feature icons and medium cards, `1rem` (16px) for large panels and dashboard previews, `9999px` for badges and pills.

**Spacing scale:** Sections use `6rem` vertical padding. Containers max at `1200px` with `1.5rem` horizontal padding. Card padding at `1.25rem`–`2rem`. Grid gaps at `2rem`.

## Accessibility Notes

- Dark mode responds to `prefers-color-scheme: dark`.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` should be respected for all non-essential animations (pulse, slide-in, hover transforms).
- Maintain WCAG 2.1 AA contrast ratios. The existing slate/blue palette generally satisfies this; test custom combinations.
- Interactive elements must have visible focus states (default browser or custom `outline: 2px solid var(--accent)`).
